About Me

I was born in a small town in northern Wisconsin and went to college at UW-Platteville, where I completed my bachelor of science degree in engineering physics. Throughout the curriculum, I took courses on topics such as C++, Mathematica, calculus, statics and dynamics, public speaking, optics, EM fields, quantum mechanics, and, of course, physics.

Out of school, I was offered a test engineer position with the same company I had an internship with, SSI Technologies, LLC. This position gave me an opportunity to learn a new programming language: LabVIEW. Despite having a graphical programming interface, it employs most of the core programming disciplines required for other languages.

Realizing my love for learning and working with new programming languages, I began to dabble with Python, which was inexplicably fun for me; it felt like the Wild West. Eventually, this led me to explore web development, culminating in my enrollment in the UW-Extension Online Coding Bootcamp.
